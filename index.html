Part A: Grammar Guide

1. SO

SO + Adjective / Adverb

Used to mean "very" or to express an extreme degree, focusing purely on the descriptive word.

  • The news spread so quickly across social media.
  • The interview was so boring that I changed the channel.

2. SUCH

SUCH + (a/an) + Adjective + Noun

Also means "very," but it must modify a noun phrase. It emphasizes the thing itself.

  • It was such a shocking story.
  • They are such credible journalists.

3. TOO

TOO + Adjective / Adverb

Has a negative meaning. It means "more than what is needed or acceptable."

  • There is too much fake news online.
  • It is too dangerous to report from the war zone.

4. ENOUGH

Adjective / Adverb + ENOUGH

Means "sufficient" or "the right amount." Notice that it comes after the adjective/adverb.

  • He is experienced enough to write this column.
  • Is this microphone loud enough for the broadcast?
